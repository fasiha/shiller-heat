Each of the lines below corresponds to a specific investment horizon (twenty years, fifty years, etc.). Each point on a line corresponds to the annualized excess rate of return that an investor would have seen on that date, if they had invested a fixed amount every month in the S&P 500 for every month over that horizon, assuming dividends reinvested. Breaking this down into more detail, the following chart contains

  • real—inflation-adjusted,
  • excess returns—beyond the risk-free Treasury rate (specifically, the ten-year Treasury note's annual yield),
  • of the S&P 500,
  • assuming a fixed monthly investment
  • over a long time horizon
  • with dividends reinvested.
